# Product positioning

## One-line positioning

Evotown is an evolution arena and enterprise control plane for independently deployed agent runtimes.

Runners stay where they are: employee laptops, CI, servers, containers, or research sandboxes. Evotown receives runs, artifacts, policy events, and reusable assets for evaluation, governance, and reuse.

## Product modes

| Mode | Audience | Value |
|------|----------|-------|
| Community / Research | framework authors, researchers, developers | visual arena, benchmarks, reproducible evolution experiments |
| Enterprise Control Plane | platform teams, security teams, engineering leads | fleet visibility, policy control, asset review, team-level reuse |

Both modes share the same core backend. The enterprise console should not remove the Arena; the Arena becomes the visual simulation layer.

## What Evotown is

- A runtime-neutral control plane for agent runs.
- A benchmark and evolution-effect validation environment.
- A registry for engines, runs, artifacts, and reusable assets.
- A policy and review surface for enterprise deployment.
- A visualization layer for evolution experiments.

## What Evotown is not

- Not a replacement for OpenClaw, Hermes, SkillLite, or custom runners.
- Not a mandatory model gateway.
- Not a full endpoint detection / MDM product.
- Not a cryptocurrency or virtual-currency economy.
- Not a system that automatically promotes one employee's local discovery to the whole company.

## Product principles

1. **Runtime neutrality:** integrations must not assume SkillLite as the only backend.
2. **Evidence over claims:** promoted assets need source runs, evaluation results, and review status.
3. **Control without lock-in:** enterprises should gain visibility and policy control without rewriting existing agent stacks.
4. **Arena remains memorable:** game-style visualization is a differentiator, not a toy.
